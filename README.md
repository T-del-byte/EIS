# EIS
# Objective
To evaluate the freshness and quality of apples using Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS) by analyzing their electrical impedance characteristics over time.
# Tools & Technologies
- LCR Meter IM3536
- Disposable ECG electrodes
- LCR Meter Sample Application
- Origin Pro software
- Zview software
# Working Principle
- A low-amplitude AC signal is applied to the apple using electrodes connected to an LCR meter, and the impedance is measured over a wide range of frequencies.
- The electrical impedance of the apple changes with storage time because factors such as moisture loss, cell membrane degradation, and changes in ion concentration affect its electrical properties.
- The measured impedance data (magnitude and phase) are plotted as Bode plots and Nyquist plots to observe how the electrical characteristics vary with the freshness of the apple.
- The Cole–Cole model is used to fit the experimental impedance data. It represents the apple as an equivalent electrical circuit and helps determine parameters such as resistance, capacitance, and relaxation behavior associated with the fruit tissue.
- The extracted Cole–Cole parameters are analyzed to evaluate the freshness and quality of the apple. Changes in these parameters indicate the degree of ripening or deterioration, enabling a non-destructive assessment of fruit quality.
# Constrction
- An LCR meter is connected to two electrodes, which are inserted into the apple sample to apply a small AC voltage over a range of frequencies.
- The electrodes act as the interface between the LCR meter and the fruit, allowing electrical signals to pass through the apple tissue.
- The LCR meter measures the electrical impedance (resistance and reactance) of the apple at different frequencies.
- The measured impedance data is transferred to a computer and analyzed using software to obtain impedance plots and equivalent circuit parameters.
- The impedance characteristics are compared over different storage days to evaluate changes in the apple's freshness and quality.
# Output
The project resulted in the creation of a reliable EIS database that can be used for future fruit quality assessment and research.
